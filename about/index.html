About the Candidate

Meet Cammie Campbell

Her Story

A Lifetime of Service to the Probate Court

Cammie Campbell is a dedicated public servant with more than three decades of experience in the probate court system. Throughout her career, she has built a reputation for professionalism, compassion, and a deep commitment to serving individuals and families during some of life's most difficult moments.

"She has dedicated her life to guiding people through those moments with steady leadership and care." — A Hallmark of Cammie's Career
Three Decades of Experience

Inside the Probate Court

Cammie has spent over 30 years working alongside some of South Carolina's most respected probate judges, as well as their associate judges. She has served the court in a range of specialized roles, gaining a comprehensive, firsthand understanding of the probate process from every angle.

Judges She Has Served Alongside

  • The Honorable Catherine Kennedy
  • The Honorable Amy W. McCulloch
  • The Honorable Daniel Eckstrom

Roles She Has Held

  • Senior Estate Clerk
  • Director of Commitment
  • Guardianship and Conservatorship Clerk
  • Senior Paralegal

In each of these roles, Cammie has worked closely with judges, attorneys, and members of the community — learning the system not from the outside, but from inside the courtroom every day.

Education & Approach

Built on Hard Work and Integrity

While working full-time, Cammie earned her Associate Degree in Public Service with a focus in Paralegal Studies from Midlands Technical College in 1998. Her career has been defined by a hands-on approach to helping individuals navigate complex legal matters with clarity, dignity, and respect.

She understands that probate court is often encountered during times of loss, uncertainty, or transition — and she has dedicated her life to guiding people through those moments with steady leadership and care.

Known for her calm demeanor and strong sense of integrity, Cammie is committed to upholding the highest standards of accountability in the courtroom. Her experience, combined with her servant's heart, has allowed her to consistently provide high-quality legal support while treating every person she encounters with compassion.

Family & Faith

Rooted in Lexington County

Outside of her professional life, Cammie is a devoted wife to her husband, Freddie, and a proud mother of five. Their family farm is located in Richland County, where they have spent years raising their children and building a life centered around faith, hard work, and service.

Today, Cammie and her family call Lexington County home. As their sons take on a greater role in farm operations, Cammie and her husband are restoring a cherished family home on the lake — one that has been in their family since the 1960s — continuing a legacy that reflects their deep commitment to family and community.

Cammie is proud to serve Lexington County and remains committed to ensuring the probate court operates with fairness, efficiency, and compassion for every person it serves.

What Drives Her

Cammie's Core Values

The principles that will guide her service as Probate Judge.

People First

Every decision should be guided by what is best for the individuals and families we serve. Cammie is committed to ensuring the Probate Court remains focused on people — not process — during life's most important moments.

Integrity

The Probate Court requires trust. Cammie believes in honesty, accountability, and doing what is right — always. She is committed to upholding the highest ethical standards in every decision and interaction.

Compassion

Many who come before the Probate Court are facing difficult and emotional circumstances. Cammie believes every individual deserves to be treated with dignity, respect, and genuine care.

Accountability

Cammie believes the court should operate with transparency and responsibility. She is committed to ensuring processes are clear, decisions are thoughtful, and the public can have confidence in how the court serves them.
